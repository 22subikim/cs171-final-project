Exponential Growth

Rather than responding appropriately to the signals that control normal cell behavior, cancer cells grow and divide in an uncontrolled manner, invading normal tissues and organs and eventually spreading throughout the body.

In only 20 rounds of cell division, one cell grows to be more than one million cells!

Exponential growth matters a lot because the earlier tumors are smaller than later tumors.

If you let cancer cells divide more, the tumor size can become very large, very quickly.

The smaller the tumor

the easier it is for doctors to treat

Unexplained Weight Loss

Unexplained weight loss is associated with cancers of the esophagus, stomach, lung, or pancreas.

Fever

Generally, all cancer patients will have a fever at some point as the tumor affects the immune system.

Fatigue

It’s a significant cancer symptom common in the early stages of leukemia and some stomach or colon cancers.

Pain

Particular pains are usually signs of cancer, such as testicular or bone cancer.

Skin Changes

The odds of eliminating various types of skin cancer are incredibly high when detected early.

Changes in Bowel Habits

Long-term constipation, diarrhea, or a change in stool size may be a sign of colon cancer.

Unusual Bleeding or Discharge

Coughing up blood could be a sign of lung cancer. Dark, black, or blood in the stool could be a symptom of rectal or colon cancer.

Anemia

Cancers like leukemia, lymphoma, and multiple myeloma can damage your marrow.

Other Symptoms

Some other symptoms to look out for are breast changes, neurological problems, eating problems, and etc.

No symptom should be ignored

Early warning signs of cancer are often similar to other common illnesses
making them easy to overlook.
Some common cancer symptoms include extreme fatigue, fever, and unexplained weight loss.

The earlier treatment begins, the better the chance of full recovery.

Cancer screening is looking for cancer before a person has any symptoms.
Screening tests can help find cancer at an early stage, before symptoms appear. When abnormal tissue or cancer is found early, it may be easier to treat or cure. By the time symptoms appear, the cancer may have grown and spread. This can make the cancer harder to treat or cure.
It is important to remember that when your doctor suggests a screening test, it does not always mean he or she thinks you have cancer. Screening tests are done when you have no cancer symptoms.
